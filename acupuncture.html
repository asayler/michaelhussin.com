Acupuncture

Early Chinese doctors felt that there was a life force they called Qi (pronounced ‘chee’) inherent in nature which also flowed through precise pathways in the body creating acupuncture ‘points’ at the skin surface. Through the use of hair-thin disposable needles, pressure, heat, or herbs this energy is thought to be brought into balance thus engendering health, well-being and ease from pain.

Included among the list of conditions identified by the National Institutes for Health and the World Health Organization as being responsive to treatment by acupuncture and Chinese medicine are such diverse conditions as:

  • Headaches
  • Various musculo-skeletal disorders (back, neck, knee, elbow, etc)
  • Sciatica
  • TMJ
  • Immune system disorders
  • Gastro-intestinal disorders
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Menstrual irregularities
  • Menopausal syndrome
  • Induction of labor
  • Malposition of fetus
  • Allergies
  • Mood disorders
  • Respiratory disorders
  • Leucopenia
  • Neurological disorders

This is only a partial list of conditions for which acupuncture and Chinese Medicine can be applied.
